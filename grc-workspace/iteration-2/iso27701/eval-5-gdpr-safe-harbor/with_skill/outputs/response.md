# ISO 27701 Certification as Evidence of GDPR Compliance

## The Critical Distinction: Evidence vs. Safe Harbor

**ISO 27701 is not a GDPR safe harbor.**

This must be stated explicitly and understood clearly before relying on ISO 27701 certification in any regulatory context. Certification does not:
- Guarantee GDPR compliance
- Shield an organization from regulatory enforcement or fines
- Constitute a legal presumption of compliance with GDPR
- Prevent a supervisory authority from investigating or taking enforcement action

What ISO 27701 certification *does* provide — and this is genuinely significant — is strong, documented evidence that your organization has implemented systematic technical and organisational measures (TOMs) for privacy protection. In the context of a regulatory investigation, this evidence is valuable and will be considered favorably. But it is not a defense in itself, and it does not cover every GDPR obligation.

This document explains exactly what ISO 27701 certification demonstrates, what it does not cover, and how to use it effectively in a regulatory investigation context.

---

## Why ISO 27701 Is Not a GDPR Safe Harbor

### 1. It Is Not an Approved Article 42 Certification Scheme

GDPR Article 42 establishes a certification mechanism: supervisory authorities may approve certification schemes that, when an organization holds certification, create a presumption of compliance with the certified obligations. Approved Article 42 schemes carry specific legal weight within GDPR's compliance framework.

**ISO 27701 has not been approved as a formal Article 42 GDPR certification scheme in most EU member states.** Without that approval, ISO 27701 certification carries no legal presumption of compliance under GDPR. It cannot be invoked as an Article 42 certification for the purpose of demonstrating compliance with GDPR controller or processor obligations.

The UK ICO has indicated that ISO 27701 may be recognized as a privacy seal mechanism under UK GDPR Article 42, which gives it somewhat stronger standing in UK regulatory proceedings — but this is a UK-specific position and does not apply across EU member states.

### 2. It Is Not a Complete Map of GDPR Obligations

ISO 27701 is a management system standard — it provides a framework of controls and practices. GDPR is a legal instrument with specific obligations that go beyond what any management system standard can fully address:

| GDPR Obligation | ISO 27701 Coverage | Gap |
|----------------|-------------------|-----|
| Mandatory DPO appointment (Art. 37) | Requires privacy roles (Clause 5) | Does not mandate DPO appointment; legal criteria for when DPO is required are GDPR-specific |
| Children's consent (Art. 8) | Consent controls (A.1.2.4/A.1.2.5) | No dedicated control for age verification or parental consent |
| Special category lawful basis (Art. 9(2)) | Lawful basis (A.1.2.3) | The specific Art. 9(2) conditions require legal analysis, not just a control |
| Supervisory authority consultation (Art. 36) | DPIA (A.1.2.6, indirectly) | Mandatory consultation after high-residual-risk DPIA not explicitly required by the standard |
| Supervisory authority cooperation (Art. 31) | Not addressed | Outside scope of the standard |
| Specific notification timeframes | Incident controls (A.3.11/A.3.12) | 72-hour breach notification window is a legal requirement; the standard does not specify this timeframe |
| Legal bases for national derogations | Not addressed | Member state law derogations are outside the scope of an international standard |
| Binding corporate rules (Art. 47) | Referenced in A.1.5.2 | No dedicated BCR control |

### 3. Scope Limitations

ISO 27701 certification covers only the defined PIMS scope. An organization may hold a valid certificate, but if certain business units, systems, or processing activities are outside the certified scope, those areas receive **no certification coverage**. A regulator investigating a data breach or complaint in an out-of-scope area cannot rely on the certificate as evidence for that area.

### 4. Historical Violations Are Not Remedied

Achieving certification after a breach or compliance failure does not remedy the historical violation. Regulators assess compliance at the time of the breach or at the time the violation occurred, not at the time certification was obtained. Post-incident certification may demonstrate commitment to improvement but does not negate the pre-certification failure.

## What ISO 27701 Certification Does Provide in a Regulatory Investigation

Despite the limitations above, ISO 27701 certification provides genuinely useful and impactful evidence in a regulatory investigation. Regulators — including EU supervisory authorities and the UK ICO — have recognized ISO 27701 as a meaningful indicator of a mature privacy programme.

### 1. Evidence of Technical and Organisational Measures (TOMs) — GDPR Articles 24 and 32

GDPR Articles 24 and 32 require controllers and processors to implement "appropriate technical and organisational measures" to ensure and demonstrate compliance, and to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk. ISO 27701 certification provides:

- A certificate and audit report demonstrating that an accredited third party has independently verified that controls are in place
- A Statement of Applicability showing which controls are implemented and how they address privacy risks
- A documented privacy risk assessment and treatment methodology showing systematic risk management
- Evidence of management commitment, internal audit, and continual improvement

This is the strongest form of TOM evidence available. In an enforcement context, it demonstrates that the organization did not simply ignore privacy — it invested in a systematic, independently audited compliance programme.

### 2. Accountability Evidence — GDPR Article 5(2)

GDPR Article 5(2) — the accountability principle — requires the controller to be responsible for, and able to demonstrate compliance with, the data protection principles. The PIMS management system creates precisely the accountability paper trail that this requires:

- Privacy Policy demonstrating top management commitment
- Records of Processing Activities (RoPA) showing systematic documentation
- DPIA records showing proactive risk assessment
- Data Subject Rights handling records showing operational compliance
- Internal audit records showing ongoing monitoring
- Management review records showing governance oversight
- Corrective action records showing improvement processes

Together, these demonstrate a systematic accountability programme, not ad hoc compliance.

### 3. Mitigating Factor in Regulatory Proceedings

EU supervisory authorities have discretion in setting fines under GDPR Article 83. The factors they must consider include:

- "Any relevant previous infringements by the controller or processor" (Art. 83(2)(e))
- "The degree of responsibility of the controller or processor taking into account technical and organisational measures implemented" (Art. 83(2)(d))
- "Any action taken by the controller or processor to mitigate the damage" (Art. 83(2)(c))

ISO 27701 certification is directly relevant to Art. 83(2)(d): it demonstrates that the organization implemented systematic technical and organisational measures. In multiple enforcement proceedings across EU member states, regulators have cited the presence (or absence) of a systematic privacy programme as a material factor in determining fines. A certified, mature PIMS is evidence in your favor.

### 4. Processor Due Diligence Evidence — GDPR Article 28

If your organization is a processor, your ISO 27701 certification provides customers (controllers) with evidence that you meet GDPR Article 28 requirements. In a regulatory investigation of a controller where your processing is involved, the controller can point to your certification as evidence that they exercised appropriate due diligence in selecting a processor with sufficient guarantees under Art. 28(1).

## Summary: What to Say — and Not Say — to Regulators

| Claim | Accurate? | Recommended Approach |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|
| "We are ISO 27701 certified, therefore we are GDPR compliant" | No | Never say this |
| "We hold ISO 27701 certification, which provides evidence of systematic technical and organisational measures" | Yes | This is the accurate framing |
| "Our ISO 27701 certification was granted by an accredited certification body that independently verified our privacy controls" | Yes | Accurate and impactful |
| "ISO 27701 certification satisfies our GDPR obligation under Article 42" | No | This is inaccurate in most EU jurisdictions |
| "ISO 27701 certification demonstrates our accountability under Article 5(2) and our implementation of TOMs under Articles 24 and 32" | Yes | This is the legally accurate and valuable claim |
| "ISO 27701 certification shows we take privacy seriously and have invested in a systematic, independently audited programme" | Yes | Appropriate for regulatory and public communications |

## Practical Recommendations for Using ISO 27701 in Regulatory Contexts

1. **Pair certification with legal analysis**: Do not treat ISO 27701 as a substitute for legal review of GDPR obligations. Use it as the operational backbone of your compliance programme while ensuring legal counsel addresses the gaps identified above.

2. **Maintain comprehensive scope**: Maximize the PIMS scope to cover as many processing activities as possible. Certification value diminishes with a narrow scope.

3. **Maintain current certification**: An expired or lapsed certificate provides little regulatory value. Keep the PIMS surveillance audits current.

4. **Document the link between controls and GDPR**: Your SoA and DPIA records should explicitly reference the GDPR articles they address. This makes the evidence chain clear to regulators.

5. **Do not rely on certification alone for high-risk processing**: If you conduct high-risk processing (special categories, large-scale profiling, systematic monitoring), conduct DPIAs and consider supervisory authority consultation proactively. Certification does not substitute for these obligations.

6. **Keep breach response records**: If a breach occurs, the quality of your response (72-hour notification, individual notification, remediation) is assessed independently of certification. Your PIMS incident response records will be scrutinized.

7. **Use certification in procurement and contracting**: This is where ISO 27701 certification has the clearest, most direct commercial value — as evidence to B2B customers that you meet Article 28 processor requirements.

---

## The Bottom Line

ISO 27701 certification is one of the strongest voluntary privacy compliance instruments available to organizations today. It is recognized by regulators, valued by enterprise customers, and provides genuine, documented evidence of a mature privacy programme. In a regulatory investigation, a well-maintained ISO 27701 certification — especially one with current surveillance audit records, comprehensive scope, and a documented management system — will be viewed favorably and can meaningfully influence enforcement outcomes.

But it is not a safe harbor. It does not guarantee compliance, does not invoke a legal presumption of compliance, and does not substitute for addressing the specific GDPR obligations that the standard does not fully cover. The correct framing — internally and when engaging with regulators — is that ISO 27701 certification is evidence of a systematic and independently audited approach to privacy management, not proof of perfect GDPR compliance.
