# CCPA/CPRA Applicability and Core Obligations

## Are You Subject to CCPA/CPRA?

**Yes, your company is subject to CCPA as amended by CPRA.**

The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended and expanded by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA, effective January 1, 2023), applies to for-profit businesses that do business in California and meet **any one** of the following thresholds:

| Threshold | Your Company | Met? |
|---|---|---|
| Annual gross revenues exceeding $25 million | $30M annual revenue | **YES** |
| Buys, sells, or shares personal information of 100,000+ consumers or households annually | ~80,000 California customers | No (below threshold) |
| Derives 50%+ of annual revenue from selling or sharing consumers' personal information | Not specified | Unknown |

**Your company meets the first threshold** — annual gross revenues of $30 million exceed the $25 million threshold. This alone is sufficient to trigger CCPA/CPRA applicability, regardless of the number of consumers whose data you process.

## Core Obligations Under CCPA/CPRA

### 1. Privacy Notice / Privacy Policy Requirements

You must provide consumers with a **comprehensive privacy notice** at or before the point of collection that discloses:

- Categories of personal information collected
- Purposes for which each category is collected and used
- Categories of third parties with whom personal information is shared
- Consumer rights and how to exercise them
- Retention periods (or criteria used to determine retention)
- Whether personal information is sold or shared (and a link to opt out if so)

Your privacy policy must be updated **at least every 12 months**.

### 2. Consumer Rights You Must Honor

CCPA/CPRA grants California consumers the following rights, which you are obligated to fulfill:

- **Right to Know**: Consumers can request disclosure of what personal information you have collected about them, the sources, purposes, and any third parties it has been shared with (covering the past 12 months, or up to 24 months under CPRA).
- **Right to Delete**: Consumers can request deletion of their personal information, subject to limited exceptions (e.g., completing a transaction, security purposes, legal obligations).
- **Right to Correct**: Under CPRA, consumers can request correction of inaccurate personal information you hold about them.
- **Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing**: If you sell or share personal information, consumers have the right to opt out. You must display a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link prominently.
- **Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information**: Consumers can direct you to limit the use of sensitive personal information (e.g., precise geolocation, financial account data, health data, biometrics) to only what is necessary for providing services.
- **Right to Non-Discrimination**: You cannot penalize consumers for exercising their CCPA/CPRA rights (e.g., no denying goods, charging different prices, or providing a lower quality of service).

### 3. Request Handling Requirements

- Establish and maintain **at least two designated methods** for submitting consumer requests (e.g., a toll-free phone number and a web form).
- Respond to verified consumer requests **within 45 days** (extendable by another 45 days with notice).
- Verify the identity of the requestor before fulfilling requests.
- Provide requested information **free of charge**, up to twice per 12-month period.
- Maintain records of consumer requests and responses for **24 months**.

### 4. Contracts with Service Providers, Contractors, and Third Parties

- Enter into **written contracts** with all service providers and contractors who process personal information on your behalf. These contracts must include CPRA-mandated terms restricting use of personal information, prohibiting sales or sharing, and requiring compliance and cooperation with consumer rights.
- Conduct reasonable **due diligence** on service providers and take action if you discover they are misusing personal information.

### 5. Data Minimization and Purpose Limitation

Under CPRA:
- Collect only the personal information **reasonably necessary** for the disclosed purposes.
- Do not use personal information for purposes that are **incompatible** with the disclosed purpose of collection.

### 6. Data Retention Limitations

- Retain personal information **no longer than reasonably necessary** for the disclosed purpose.
- Disclose your retention periods (or the criteria used to determine them) in your privacy policy.

### 7. Security Requirements

- Implement and maintain **reasonable security measures** appropriate to the nature of the personal information you collect (this obligation predates CCPA and is codified in California Civil Code § 1798.81.5).
- CPRA's private right of action (and statutory damages of $100–$750 per consumer per incident) applies specifically to **data breaches** resulting from failure to implement reasonable security.

### 8. Sensitive Personal Information

CPRA introduces special rules for **Sensitive Personal Information (SPI)**, which includes:
- Social Security numbers, driver's license numbers, financial account credentials
- Precise geolocation
- Race, ethnicity, religion
- Health, medical, and genetic data
- Biometric data used for identification
- Sexual orientation or sex life
- Communications content (unless you are the intended recipient)

If you collect SPI, you must disclose this and honor consumers' right to limit its use.

## Enforcement

- Enforcement is handled by the **California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA)** and the **California Attorney General**.
- Civil penalties up to **$2,500 per unintentional violation** and **$7,500 per intentional violation** (or involving a minor's data).
- Private right of action for **data breaches** only, with statutory damages of $100–$750 per consumer per incident.

## Recommended Immediate Next Steps

1. **Audit your data practices**: Map what personal information you collect, why, where it is stored, and with whom it is shared.
2. **Update your privacy policy** to meet CCPA/CPRA disclosure requirements.
3. **Implement consumer request workflows**: Set up intake, verification, response, and record-keeping processes.
4. **Review vendor contracts**: Ensure all service providers have CPRA-compliant data processing agreements.
5. **Assess sensitive personal information**: Identify whether you collect any SPI and add appropriate controls and disclosures.
6. **Implement a "Do Not Sell or Share" mechanism** if you sell or share personal information.
7. **Train staff** on consumer rights obligations and your internal response procedures.
